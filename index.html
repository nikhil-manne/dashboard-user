Project Overview

How the Smart Kiosk System Works

A step-by-step breakdown of the end-to-end complaint lifecycle

01
Step 1 📍

Kiosks at Key City Locations

Digitally connected kiosks are placed across strategic public locations — bus stations, markets, government offices, and public squares — ensuring accessibility for every citizen without requiring them to travel to a police station.

02
Step 2 🗂️

Select a Complaint Category

At the kiosk, the citizen selects a complaint category from a clearly organized menu — covering harassment, domestic violence, cybercrime, missing persons, public safety, and more — guiding them through a structured intake process.

03
Step 3 🧑‍💼

Enter Details or Get Officer Assistance

Citizens enter complaint details through a simple, intuitive interface. An assigned officer at the kiosk location is available to assist those who need help — ensuring no one is turned away due to literacy or language barriers.

04
Step 4 ⚡

Instant Submission to Central Backend

Upon submission, complaint data is instantly transmitted to a centralized backend server. The server processes, logs, timestamps, and validates the complaint in real time — creating a permanent, immutable record of the grievance.

05
Step 5 🔀

Auto-Routing to Appropriate Department

The system intelligently routes each complaint to the correct police or administrative department based on the complaint's location and category — eliminating manual sorting, reducing response delays, and ensuring no complaint falls through the cracks.

06
Step 6 🖨️

Printed Acknowledgement & Tracking ID

A physical receipt is immediately printed for the citizen, containing a unique Tracking ID. This enables follow-up at any time through the online tracking portal or at any kiosk — ensuring full transparency and accountability.

07
Step 7 📊

Real-Time Dashboard for Authorities

Police officers, commissionerate staff, and administrators can monitor, respond to, and manage complaints in real time through role-based dashboards — enabling faster resolution, escalation management, and statewide oversight.

08
Step 8 🔍

Citizen Complaint Tracking

Citizens can check complaint status at any time using their Tracking ID — through the web portal or at a kiosk — receiving real-time status updates as their complaint moves through review, investigation, and resolution stages.

The institutional framework behind the Smart Kiosk System

The Smart Public Complaint Kiosk System is developed and deployed as a flagship technology initiative of the Women Safety Wing (WSW), Telangana Police. The Wing is a fully dedicated department of the Telangana State Police committed to the safety, dignity, and empowerment of women and children across the state.

By placing public-facing kiosks at key city locations, the WSW extends its reach beyond police stations and helpline numbers — making complaint filing accessible for every citizen regardless of digital literacy, mobility, or location. The system directly supports the Wing's mandate of Prevention, Protection, and Prosecution.

The WSW was formally established on 8 March 2018 — International Women's Day — and operates multiple specialized modules including SHE Teams, Bharosa Centres, the Cyber Module (CybHER), Anti-Human Trafficking Units, the NRI Women Safety Cell, and now the Smart Kiosk Complaint Network.

Official Website: For the full Women Safety Wing portal, resources, laws, and support services, visit womensafetywing.telangana.gov.in
SHE Teams — Est. 24 October 2014 Plain-clothes women police officers at public spaces preventing harassment. 341+ active teams statewide — India's pioneering initiative.
Bharosa Centres — One-Stop Support Integrated crisis centres for survivors offering medical, legal, psychological, and shelter support under one roof. Operational in 12+ districts.
Cyber Module & CybHER Dedicated cybercrime support for women — online harassment, stalking, matrimonial fraud. CybHER launched 15 July 2020.
Anti-Human Trafficking Unit (AHTU) Specialized units to detect, investigate, and rescue victims of trafficking across Telangana.
NRI Women Safety Cell — Est. 17 July 2019 Handles crimes against women by NRI spouses, including matrimonial fraud, desertion, and cross-border domestic violence.
