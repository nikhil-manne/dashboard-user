About the Programme

About Women Safety Wing, Telangana

Who we are, our history, and our mandate

The Women Safety Wing (WSW) is a fully dedicated department of the Telangana State Police, working round the clock to ensure the safety, dignity, and empowerment of women and children across the state. Carved out from the Women Protection Cell, CID, the WSW operates from independent premises with its own cadre strength and a very specific mandate.

The Wing handles investigation into all crimes against women — including prevention of trafficking, sexual offences, domestic violence, NRI-related issues, cybercrime, and juvenile matters. It brings together law enforcement, legal support, psychological counselling, and medical aid under a single institutional umbrella.

The Wing was formally established on 8 March 2018 — International Women's Day — reflecting the Government of Telangana's commitment to giving women's safety a dedicated, institutional, and empowered response mechanism. Today, WSW operates multiple specialized modules including SHE Teams, Bharosa Centres, Cyber Module, Anti-Human Trafficking Unit, NRI Cell, and the Missing Persons Monitoring Cell.

Establishment: Women Safety Wing was officially established on 8 March 2018, carved out from the Women Protection Cell, CID of Telangana Police. India's first Child Friendly Court was also set up on 7 April 2018 in Hyderabad City.
SHE Teams — Est. 24 October 2014 Plain-clothes women police officers deployed at public spaces to prevent and act against harassment. Pioneering initiative in India, now with 341+ active teams statewide.
Bharosa Centres — Integrated Support One-stop crisis centres for survivors of sexual violence and domestic abuse, offering medical, legal, psychological, and shelter support under one roof. Operational in 12+ districts.
Cyber Module & CybHER Dedicated cybercrime support for women — from online harassment and stalking to matrimonial fraud. CybHER launched 15 July 2020 to handle digital crimes against women and children.
Anti-Human Trafficking Unit (AHTU) Specialized units to detect, investigate, and rescue victims of human trafficking across Telangana and coordinate with national agencies.
NRI Women Safety Cell — Est. 17 July 2019 Handles cases of crimes against women by NRI spouses or relatives, including matrimonial fraud, desertion, and cross-border domestic violence.
Why This Programme

Motive & Objectives of Women Safety Wing

The mandate that drives every initiative

01

Dedicated Institutional Response

Prior to WSW, women's safety cases were handled by general police divisions without specialized training, resources, or focus. The Wing was created to provide a dedicated, sensitive, and expert response to crimes against women and children — eliminating procedural gaps and ensuring survivor-centric processes.

02

Prevention Before Prosecution

The WSW adopts a proactive stance — through SHE Teams in public hotspots, Safety Clubs in schools and colleges, and community awareness programmes — the Wing works to prevent crimes from occurring in the first place, rather than merely reacting after the fact.

03

Holistic Survivor Support

Bharosa Centres provide integrated support — legal advice, medical examination, psychological counselling, shelter, and police assistance — ensuring that a survivor never has to navigate the system alone. The approach treats rehabilitation and empowerment as equally important as justice.

04

Technology as an Enabler

From the T-Safe app and Hawkeye platform to the SHE Team mobile application and the Integrated Complaint Portal, WSW leverages digital tools to make women's safety accessible, traceable, and accountable — lowering barriers to reporting and improving response times.

05

Accountability & Transparency

This Integrated Complaint Management Portal was developed to ensure that every complaint filed at a Booth is tracked through commissionerate review and administrative oversight — creating an auditable, transparent system with end-to-end accountability from registration to resolution.

06

Empowerment Through Awareness

An aware citizen is an empowered citizen. WSW runs safety clubs, CybHER awareness sessions, and community outreach to equip women and children with the knowledge to identify risks, access legal rights, and seek timely help — building a culture of safety from the ground up.

Programmes & Initiatives

Key Modules of Women Safety Wing

Specialized units working to protect, prevent, and empower

SHE Teams

Plain-clothes women police officers at public hotspots — markets, bus stands, colleges — to deter, detect and act against sexual harassment and eve-teasing. Operational since October 2014.

Bharosa Centres

Integrated one-stop support centres for survivors of sexual violence and domestic abuse, providing medical, legal, psychological and shelter services in 12+ districts of Telangana.

Cyber Module & CybHER

Handles online harassment, stalking, cyberbullying, and digital crimes against women and children. CybHER specifically addresses cyber safety awareness for women and girls.

Anti-Human Trafficking Unit

Specialized investigation units that work to prevent trafficking, conduct rescue operations, rehabilitate victims, and prosecute perpetrators of human trafficking.

Domestic Violence Module

Dedicated officers and support staff trained in domestic violence response. Includes CDEW — Centre for Development and Empowerment of Women — for survivors' reintegration.

NRI Cell & Sahas

NRI Cell handles cross-border matrimonial crimes. Sahas focuses on workplace sexual harassment prevention and the POSH Act. Both are nationally recognized models.
